Rulebook and Foundations of Life

How A Turn Works

1. You collect income and cover recurring bills.

2. Your credit shifts based on payments, utilization, debt pressure, and negative marks.

3. You roll the dice and land on a life, credit, housing, transport, crisis, or growth space.

4. Your choices shape savings, debt, and milestone progress.

Foundations of Life

Stability: housing and an emergency fund.

Mobility: transportation that is useful and not a trap.

Growth: a side hustle, investing, or another future asset.

Security: strong credit and surviving setbacks without collapse.

How You Win

Reach Financial Freedom by finishing all six goals shown in the roadmap panel: stability, mobility, growth, security, savings, and debt control.

Table Talk

Before you roll

How Credit Climb actually works

Three things to know before turn one. Re-open this any time with the ? in the top right.

1

Every choice is a trade-off

The fun option usually costs you Cash or Credit. The boring option usually costs you Wellbeing. There is no "right" answer — only what you're willing to give up.

2

Building credit is slow. Breaking it is fast.

Paying on time gives you small gains (+2 to +5 points). One missed payment can drop you 30–60 points. Don't expect to recover quickly.

3

Your profile shapes the system

The five profiles aren't difficulty settings — they're different starting points. The same prompt can mean very different things depending on what you started with.
